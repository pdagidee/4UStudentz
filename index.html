Cyber Attacks Are On The Rise

You are a target!

Did you know that university students are attractive targets for cyber-attacks?

Why you are a target

  • Lack of Awareness: While students may be aware of cybercrime, they are not educated enough on the severity of cyberattacks, the variety of ways in which they can occur (e.g phishing) and how to prevent them.
  • High Internet Usage: Students spend lots of time online, across different platforms (university websites, social media, etc), which cybercriminals will take advantage of.
  • Access to Valuable Data: Through students, sensitive information from university databases can be uncovered. This includes banking details, addresses, etc.

Common cyber-attacks

  • Phishing: Phishing is when criminals use scam messages to trick people into sending sensitive information, pay money, or click on a link which contains a virus or takes you to a fraudulent website. Phishing may happen by various means, most commonly by email, text (smishing) or voice call (vishing). Most phishing attackers send high numbers of messages and expect success in relatively few cases.

  • Trojan Horses: With Trojan Horses, a malware that typically gets hidden as an attachment in an email or a free-to-download file, then transfers onto the user’s device. Once downloaded, the malicious code will execute the task the attacker designed it for, such as gain backdoor access to corporate systems, spy on users’ online activity, or steal sensitive data.

  • Ransomware: Ransomware a type of malware which prevents you from accessing your device and the data stored on it, usually by encrypting your files. A criminal group will then demand a ransom in exchange for decryption.
